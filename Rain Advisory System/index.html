Field-Deployed Offline Micro-Weather Based Rain Advisory System for Agriculture

About Sensors

About the Project

Field-Deployed Offline Micro-Weather Based Rain Advisory System for Agriculture is a practical and farmer-centric project designed to provide reliable rain advisories directly at the field level without depending on internet connectivity.

In many agricultural regions, especially rural areas, weather information from online services is often inaccurate for local conditions or unavailable due to poor network coverage. This system overcomes that limitation by using on-field micro-weather sensing and local data processing to generate rain advisories specific to the crop location.

The system continuously monitors key environmental parameters such as temperature, humidity, atmospheric pressure, soil moisture, and rainfall using sensors connected to an ESP32 microcontroller. The collected data is processed offline to analyze short-term weather behavior and predict the likelihood of rainfall.

The advisory output is displayed instantly on an OLED screen, enabling farmers to make timely decisions related to irrigation, sowing, fertilization, and crop protection.

By operating completely offline, the system ensures low cost, fast response, and high reliability in real-world field conditions. This project demonstrates an effective application of embedded systems and micro-weather analysis to support sustainable and smart agricultural practices.

Working of the System (Detailed)

The Field-Deployed Offline Micro-Weather Based Rain Advisory System for Agriculture is designed to operate continuously in real agricultural field conditions without depending on internet connectivity. The system focuses on collecting localized micro-weather data and generating timely rain advisories directly at the field level.

At the initial stage, multiple environmental sensors are deployed in the agricultural field. These sensors continuously monitor critical weather parameters such as ambient temperature, relative humidity, atmospheric pressure, soil moisture content, air quality, and direct rainfall detection. Since weather conditions can vary significantly within short distances, on-field sensing ensures higher accuracy compared to generalized online weather forecasts.

All sensor data is fed into the ESP32 microcontroller, which acts as the core processing unit of the system. The ESP32 reads the sensor values at regular intervals and performs basic data validation and filtering to remove noise or abnormal readings. This ensures that only reliable data is used for further analysis.

The processed sensor values are then analyzed using predefined threshold levels and micro-weather decision logic stored within the microcontroller. By observing changes in temperature, humidity, pressure trends, and soil moisture conditions, the system evaluates the probability of rainfall in the immediate environment.

When the calculated parameters indicate a high likelihood of rain, or when the rain sensor directly detects precipitation, the system immediately generates a rain advisory. This advisory helps farmers make timely decisions such as postponing irrigation, scheduling fertilizer application, or protecting crops from potential damage.

The final advisory output, along with real-time sensor readings, is displayed on an OLED screen. This visual display allows farmers to quickly understand the current weather status and predicted rain conditions without needing smartphones or internet-based applications.
